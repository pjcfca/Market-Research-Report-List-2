<p><p>VGA cables are categorized into three main types based on the refresh rate they support: 60Hz, 70Hz, and 85Hz. The refresh rate, measured in hertz (Hz), indicates how many times per second the display refreshes and can impact the clarity and smoothness of the video output. The 60Hz cables are standard and suitable for most applications, while the 70Hz and 85Hz cables offer higher refresh rates for better performance, particularly in tasks that require fast motion or high-quality graphics.</p></p>
