<p><p>Underwater drones can be classified into two main types: Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUV) and Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROV). AUVs are self-propelled drones that operate without direct human control, following pre-programmed instructions to navigate underwater environments. On the other hand, ROVs are controlled by a human operator from the surface using a tether, allowing for real-time manipulation and data collection. Both AUVs and ROVs are important tools in various industries such as oil and gas, marine research, and underwater exploration.</p></p>
