<p><p>Machine loading robots come in various types based on the number of axes they operate on. 4-axis robots are capable of linear movements along three axes and a rotary movement around one axis. 5-axis robots add an extra rotary movement around a second axis, allowing for more complex movements. 6-axis robots are the most common, with additional flexibility due to a wrist-like structure. 7-axis robots offer even more precision and range of motion. Other markets may include specialized robots with unique axis configurations to meet specific industry needs.</p></p>
