<p><p>Dried vegetables are available in two main types, freeze dried vegetables and air dried vegetables. Freeze dried vegetables are preserved by freezing and then slowly removing the moisture through a process called sublimation, which helps retain most of the nutrients and flavors. Air dried vegetables are dehydrated using hot air, which removes moisture while keeping the nutritional content intact. Both types are popular in the market for their convenience, long shelf life, and retained nutrients.</p></p>
