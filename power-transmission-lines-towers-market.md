<p><p>Power transmission lines and towers are essential components of the electricity distribution system. The market is segmented into High Tension (HT), Extra High Tension (EHT), and Ultra High Tension (UHT) categories based on the voltage levels they can handle. HT lines typically carry voltages up to 33 kV, while EHT lines can carry voltages between 66 kV to 132 kV. UHT lines are capable of carrying voltages exceeding 220 kV. Each type of transmission line requires specific towers and infrastructure to efficiently transmit electricity over long distances.</p></p>
